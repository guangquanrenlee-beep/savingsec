The Honeywell HPA304 uses a **three-stage filtration system**: a pre-filter captures large airborne debris like pet fur and dust bunnies, a True HEPA filter traps 99.97% of particles as small as 0.3 microns — including pollen, mold spores, dust mite debris, and pet dander — and an activated carbon pre-filter helps reduce VOCs and household odors. **Rating: 5 / 5**

If seasonal allergies have you reaching for tissues year-round, the HPA304 is among the best non-medical interventions you can buy. The combination of high CADR and frequent air changes means pollen, ragweed, and grass allergens are physically removed from your air, not simply trapped or neutralized.

For allergy sufferers, this is a meaningful practical benefit — you are less likely to forget maintenance when you see the pre-filter visibly dirty.

**Room-level optimization tip:** Place the unit in the room where you spend the most daytime hours, not necessarily your bedroom. ## For Pet Dander — 5/5

**Rating: 5 / 5**

Pet dander particles range from 0.5 to 5 microns — well within the True HEPA capture range. The HPA304 excels here for two reasons: **high air volume** and **effective pre-filtration**.

We tested this unit in a home with two medium-sized dogs and a cat. Within 24 hours of continuous operation on Medium speed, the visible dust on horizontal surfaces was noticeably reduced — though pet owners should be realistic: no air purifier eliminates the need for regular vacuuming and surface cleaning. What the HPA304 does well is handle the airborne fraction that vacuuming cannot reach.

The pre-filter catches fur before it reaches the HEPA filter, which matters practically. In our test, the pre-filter was visibly fur-covered after 3 days and needed a quick vacuum cleaning. This is a good thing — without it, all that fur would have ended up on the HEPA element, degrading performance and shortening filter life.

**For pet owners with allergies:** This unit creates a noticeable difference in the first 24 hours. The "stale pet air" feeling in living areas — that subtle odor and heavy air sensation — drops significantly. It will not cure your allergy, but it will reduce the airborne allergen load enough that symptoms become more manageable.

## For Dust Mites — 5/5

**Rating: 5 / 5**

Dust mites are one of the most common indoor allergens, and their waste particles (5–10 microns) are readily captured by True HEPA filtration. However, it is important to understand what an air purifier can and cannot do for dust mite allergies.

**What the HPA304 does:** Captures airborne dust mite debris and fecal particles that become suspended when you walk on carpets, make the bed, or sit on upholstered furniture. This airborne fraction is what triggers allergic reactions — the particles you breathe in.

**What it does not do:** Eliminate dust mites from your mattress, pillows, or upholstery. Dust mites live deep inside fabrics and bedding, and no air purifier can reach them there. For full dust mite management, the HPA304 should be used alongside:
- **Allergen-proof mattress and pillow encasings**
- **Weekly hot-water washing (130°F / 54°C minimum) of bedding**
- **Vacuuming with a HEPA-filtered vacuum**

With this combined approach, users report a **significant reduction in morning congestion and sneezing** within 1–2 weeks.

## For Smoke Odor — 4.5/5

**Rating: 4.5 / 5**

The HPA304 handles smoke particles well — the True HEPA filter captures solid smoke particulates efficiently, and the activated carbon pre-filter provides some odor reduction. However, there are important distinctions to make.

**For cigarette, cigar, or wildfire smoke:** The HEPA element captures the solid particulate matter (ash, soot, PM2.5) with high efficiency. The activated carbon pre-filter helps with odor but is relatively thin compared to dedicated carbon filters found in units like the Coway Airmega or Winix 5500-2. For light to moderate smoke exposure, this is sufficient. For heavy, continuous smoking or extended wildfire events, you may want to supplement with a standalone carbon filter unit.

**For cooking smoke:** This is where the HPA304 shines. In an open-plan kitchen-living area, running the unit on High or Turbo during cooking captures grease particles and smoke before they settle on surfaces. After cooking, 20–30 minutes on High clears residual odors effectively.

**For VOC and chemical off-gassing:** The carbon pre-filter is too thin for effective VOC removal from paint, new furniture, or cleaning products. If VOCs are your primary concern, look for a unit with a thicker carbon filter.

**The half-point deduction:** Noise on Turbo mode (58 dB) and the limited carbon filter are the only real drawbacks for smoke handling. If noise is not a concern and smoke is occasional rather than constant, this remains a strong choice.

## Noise Levels

Noise is one of the most important practical factors in an air purifier. The HPA304 offers four speeds with clearly different noise profiles:

| Speed | Noise Level | Equivalent | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Low** | 39 dB | Quiet library, refrigerator hum | Silent enough for bedrooms, unnoticed during sleep |
| **Medium** | 47 dB | Moderate rainfall, office background | Noticeable but not intrusive; good for daytime living areas |
| **High** | 52 dB | Loud conversation, window AC unit | Pronounced; fine for TV or focused work with some masking |
| **Turbo** | 58 dB | Vacuum cleaner, busy restaurant | Loud; use only when air quality demands it — wildfire, cooking, or quick cleanup |

The unit produces a **consistent whoosh sound** rather than mechanical rattling or high-pitched whine. This is important — consistent broadband noise is far less fatiguing than variable or tonal noise. On Low, it is genuinely quiet enough for light sleepers. On Turbo, you will want to leave the room.

**Adjustment tip:** If you find Medium too loud for sleeping but Low too subtle for air cleaning, try running the unit on High for 30 minutes before bed, then switching to Low for the night. This pre-cleans the room air while you wind down, and the Low setting maintains clean air during sleep.
